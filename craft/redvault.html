Red Vault

Red Vault is a first of its kind rewards app for gamers in the MENA region. Earn rewards for buying video games at your favourite retail stores. It's that simple.

About Parent Company:

Founded in 1996, Red Entertainment Distribution is a leading distributor and marketer of entertainment products in the Middle East employing a winning combination of more than 65 industry professionals. Red solidified its status in the GCC through strong business practices, quickly becoming the benchmark for other distributors in the region. Red network covers the United Arab Emirates, the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Kuwait, Bahrain, Oman, Lebanon and Egypt.

My Role

I handled the core experience and interface design of Red Vault version 1 from August 2017 to October 2017. Later on, I assisted the development team with deliverables and iterations.

The Challenge

Scan
1. User needs to effictively scan the QR code to redeem the points.
2. Have an alternate to scanning QR code.
Inform
1. Let user know about upcoming game release.
2. Promotional offers, early access to engage the user.
Vault
1. Fetching data about user’s purchases.
2. Leveling user based on games purchased under RED Vault.
3. What is the user interested in?

The Users

Major portion of user are going to be young gaming professionals or amateurs. RED Entertainment being in business for over 2 decades need to setup a rewarding system and engage their users.
Our user base in going to be gulf countries only. Any purchases made from rest of the world are not eligible for points.

Scan

The scanning was primary feature that needed to be simple yet effective. All the scans needed to be precise and mapped with a unique end product.

The biggest prolem we were facing was the scenario of tampered QR code or where a user made a purchase earlier and misplaced the cover and was unable to go through the scanning workflow.

After some brainstorming session, we produced wireframes and went to internal testing to see if the problems were being taken care of.

Findings:
1. We needed to inform user visually of the placement of the QR code.
2. The interface needs to be self explanatory.

Output

Inform

For version 1, the idea was to let users know about upcoming game release, promotional offers, early access to gaming events. We needed an efficient way to put the content through.

During structuring this section, we took into consideration:
· The number of events happening were limited.
· In the gaming sector, users tend to flock towards quality of the game.
· Gamers tend to explore more options and are open to suggestions.

Solution
· We fetched the gaming interests from user.
· Interests along with type of console, region were considered to build feed.
· The interest are further used to show user the feed they want to see along with what’s trending. Content with less relevancy is pushed below.

Output

Vault

This was app central where everything happening was piled up. From user data, interests, consoles, alerts, reminders to get information were complied into a structure.

Breakdown

Solution

We structured the information and tested various concepts internally.
· The hybrid collapsed list and slider was finalised after clear dominance in ease of use and clear infomation access.
· Between thumnail views and large previews upfront, thumbnails clearly struck out from the response and hence the sliding interface.
· We prioritised the features via quick card sorting method and placed them accordingly in a list view.
· Alerts and settings initially placed on bottom navigation were taken to header.
· Driving factor for alert placement was limited set of notification yet it needed optimal visibility.
